COVID-19, also known as Coronavirus Disease 2019, is a contagious disease caused by the coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. The first known case was identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. The symptoms of COVID-19 can vary but often include fever, fatigue, cough, breathing difficulties, loss of smell, and loss of taste. The disease quickly spread worldwide, resulting in the COVID-19 pandemic.

Preventive measures for COVID-19 include physical distancing, wearing masks, hand hygiene, and vaccination. Several COVID-19 vaccines have been developed and distributed globally to help prevent the spread of the virus. The pandemic has caused significant social and economic disruptions worldwide, leading to lockdowns, travel restrictions, and other measures to control the spread of the virus.
